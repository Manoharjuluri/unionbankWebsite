How to Get an Education Loan for Your Higher Studies in India & Abroad Studies?


Why Is An Education Loan Becoming Necessary For Higher Education?

Education loans are now a must as the tuition fee for engineering and medical universities have risen to the point that it is no longer feasible to pay the full fee.

That's why education loans have become a necessity. A student can simply enroll in a reputable college in such a situation by obtaining an education loan in India.

Types Of Education Loans In India

Based on the location, there are two types of education loan process:

  • Education loan in India - Individuals can apply for this form of the loan if they wish to pursue their education in India. Only applicants who are accepted into Indian educational institutions and who meet all other requirements set forth by the lender will have their loan requests approved.
  • Education loan for abroad - These loans assist students in realizing their dream of attending an abroad institution to pursue their chosen course of study. Students who want to study abroad can apply for a loan that will pay for their airfare, lodging, and tuition as long as they meet the requirements.

Which Courses Are Eligible?

As long as the course is offered by an approved institution or has a high reputation, students can choose to enroll in nearly any course they want to. The types of courses that you can often apply for with a loan includes:

  1. Courses Covered For Education Loans In India
    • PhD courses
    • Graduation/PG/Diploma courses from reputed institutions & universities
    • Technical/Professional / Management courses
  2. Courses Covered For Education Loan Abroad
    • PhD courses
    • Graduation/PG degrees offered by reputed institutions/universities
    • Certified degree courses offered by CIMA, London, CPA, USA, and others

What Documents Are Required For An Education Loan Abroad & In India?

When applying for an education loan, the following documentation is required:

  • Educational certificates
  • KYC documents
  • Admission letter
  • Fee Structure
  • Income proof and KYC documents of co-applicant
